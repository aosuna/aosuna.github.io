The Potato


The potato is a starchy tuber crop. This is a highly used crop and is cultivated and harvested from different regions of the world. There are approximately 5,000 different types of species of potato, and 3,000 of those species can be found in the Andes.

The potato has a variety of nutritional values. It contains fiber, vitamins, and minerals. The skin of the potato contains 27mg of vitamin c which is 45% of the daily intake. It is known for its carbohydrate starch. The starch can protect against a variety of different things once consumed. It can prevent colon cancer and improve glucose intolerance.

There are a lot of nutritional uses for the potato. It can low cholesterol and tryglyceride concentrations. For those that are on a glycemic diet, it is recommended to not consume potatoes. In this case and many other the potato can contain may toxic compounds. The potato uses these toxins to protect itself from predators. It is not recommended to eat wild potatoes because of the high toxicity they may contain.
